# GroceryView BI Portfolio Overview

## Project summary

**GroceryView** is a retail price intelligence project that turns product-level grocery price data into business-ready analytics.

The project tracks grocery prices across chains, stores, categories, brands, and product types, then converts raw price observations into KPIs, dashboards, alerts, and business insights.

This BI portfolio version demonstrates skills relevant to **Data Analyst**, **BI Analyst**, **Reporting Analyst**, **Operations Analyst**, and **Business Analyst** roles.

## Business problem

Grocery prices change frequently across chains and product categories. Shoppers and decision-makers need a clear way to answer questions such as:

- Which chain is cheapest this week?
- How much does the same basket cost across different stores?
- Which products have the biggest price drops?
- Which categories are becoming more expensive?
- Are current prices unusually high or low compared with history?
- Which products have missing, stale, or suspicious price data?
- What actions should a shopper or business take based on the data?

## BI objective

The objective is to transform raw product and price data into a reporting system that supports:

1. **Executive summary reporting**
2. **Chain and store benchmarking**
3. **Basket cost comparison**
4. **Product price history analysis**
5. **Deal detection**
6. **Data quality monitoring**
7. **Automated weekly reporting**
8. **Actionable business recommendations**

## What makes this a strong BI portfolio project

Unlike a simple tutorial dashboard, GroceryView is based on a real-world analytical problem. It requires:

- messy product-level data
- time-varying prices
- cross-chain comparison
- product matching
- data freshness checks
- business-facing metrics
- decision-oriented reporting

That makes it closer to the type of work performed in real analyst roles.
